## The Problem: A $7B+ Pain Point No One Has Solved

Soft-tissue injuries are the single largest cause of athlete unavailability in professional sport. A hamstring tear costs a Premier League club an estimated £500,000–£1M in lost performance, medical costs, and squad disruption. Across a single season, top-flight clubs lose an average of 45–60 player-days per 25-man squad to preventable soft-tissue injuries.

The current market offers two partial solutions:

- **Wearable platforms** (HRV, sleep, GPS load monitoring) measure full-body fatigue but cannot see what is happening inside a specific muscle or joint.
- **Thermal imaging tools** capture tissue-level heat patterns but operate in isolation, with no integration into readiness scoring or clinical decision-making.

Neither solution can detect the most dangerous state in sport: the athlete in **Deceptive Readiness** — systemically recovered, feeling fit, cleared by every existing tool — but carrying hidden tissue inflammation that will become an injury under training load.

This state is invisible to every platform on the market today. NUR is the first system built to find it.

The problem does not end at injury. The decision of when to return an injured athlete to competition is currently based on time elapsed, subjective clinical feel, and how the athlete reports. This is why re-injury rates in hamstring injuries run at 14–30%. Each re-injury is the full cost repeated: weeks lost, medical expenditure doubled, squad depth further eroded. No platform on the market provides an objective, data-driven gate for return-to-play decisions. NUR does.

## The Solution: COMPASS — A New Clinical Standard

NUR is built on COMPASS, a proprietary two-axis risk framework developed by Amplifai Health that combines:

| Signal | Source | What It Reveals |
|--------|--------|-----------------|
| Systemic Readiness | Wearable data (HRV, sleep, training load) | Full-body fatigue and recovery state |
| Thermal Asymmetry Index | NUR Sensor (infrared thermal imaging) | Tissue-level inflammation and local stress |

The combination produces a single COMPASS Score (0–100) and assigns every athlete to one of four zones:

- **Safe** — Ready for full load
- **Recovery** — Systemic fatigue; load reduction recommended
- **Deceptive Readiness** — Normal wearables, elevated tissue stress; mandatory physiotherapy review
- **Danger** — Fatigued and inflamed; clinical intervention required

The Deceptive Readiness zone is NUR's core clinical insight. It is the highest-injury-risk state, it is invisible to all competitors, and detecting it is what NUR was built for.

COMPASS operates in two clinical modes:

- **Prevention mode** (default): Active athletes, uninjured — zones reflect training readiness and hidden injury risk.
- **Monitoring mode** (post-injury): Injured athletes in rehabilitation — the same dual-signal framework tracks tissue healing and governs phase-by-phase return-to-play decisions with objective thermal gates.

This dual-mode architecture means NUR is not purchased for a single use case. A club that adopts NUR for injury prevention retains it through every athlete's rehabilitation. The platform that prevents the injury also governs the return from it.

## Why No One Can Replicate This

Five structural moats protect NUR's position:

**1. Proprietary thermal-physiological integration**
Thermal imaging and wearable data must be processed together within the same scoring model to generate the Deceptive Readiness classification. Bolting a thermal camera onto an existing wearable platform does not produce this capability. The integration is the product.

**2. Clinical protocol engine, not just a risk score**
Competitors produce injury risk percentages. NUR produces deterministic zone classifications with mandatory clinical protocols attached. This is the difference between a dashboard and a decision support system — the latter is what medical and performance directors will pay for and defend to their boards.

**3. Individual athlete baselines**
NUR builds each athlete's thermal baseline from their own historical healthy scans, not just population averages. This means NUR becomes more accurate — and more valuable — the longer a team uses it. The data moat grows with tenure. Critically, these baselines are the reference point for return-to-play clearance — an athlete mid-rehabilitation cannot switch to a competing platform without losing the pre-injury thermal norm that makes RTP gates meaningful. The switching cost is uniquely high at exactly the moment it would be most disruptive.

**4. The hardware-software pairing**
The NUR Sensor creates a proprietary data acquisition layer that competitors cannot access without significant capital investment, regulatory navigation, and clinical validation work. The barrier to replication is multi-year.

**5. Evidence base**
The thermal thresholds in COMPASS are calibrated to peer-reviewed findings showing a 64% reduction in soft-tissue injury when athletes are identified and managed at the correct threshold. This is not a feature claim — it is a published clinical result that underpins every sale.

Embedded across all surfaces. Generates personalised, phased intervention plans — not generic advice, but athlete-specific protocols drawn from their actual scan data, medical history, and zone classification. Every recommendation includes a named rationale and a threshold-gated progression criteria.
